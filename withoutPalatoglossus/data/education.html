Tongue Muscles 101

Extrinsic Muscles

Extrinsic muscles move the tongue around like a puppet



Genioglossus:

posterior fibers advance the tongue, middle fibers depress the back of the tongue, and anterior fibers draw the front of the tongue down and back

Hyoglossus:

retracts and depresses the tongue

Styloglossus:

potentially elevates and retracts the tongue

Palatoglossus:

raises the back of the tongue

Intrinsic Muscles

Intrinsic muscles change the shape of the tongue from the inside



Superior Longitudinal:

curls the apex and lateral sides of the tongue upwards; when contracted with inferior longitudinal, they retract the tongue tip

Inferior Longitudinal:

curl the apex and lateral sides of the tongue downwards; when acting with the superior longitudinal, they retract the tongue tip

Transverse:

narrows the tongue body, which may result in elongation and protrusion

Vertical:

flattens and broadens the tongue body, which may result in elongation and protrusion
