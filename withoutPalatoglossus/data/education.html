Tongue Muscles 101

Extrinsic Muscles

Genioglossus:

inferior fibers protrude, middle fibers depress, and superior fibers draw the tip back

Hyoglossus:

retracts and depresses

Styloglossus:

elevates and retracts

Palatoglossus:

raises the back

Intrinsic Muscles

Superior Longitudinal:

curls the apex and lateral sides upwards; when contracted with inferior longitudinal, they shorten and retract the tongue when it is protruding.

Inferior Longitudinal:

curl the apex and lateral sides downwards; when acting with the superior longitudinal, they shorten and react the tongue when it is protruding.

Transverse:

elongates and protrudes

Vertical:

flatten and broaden
